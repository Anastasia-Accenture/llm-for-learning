# Research Findings: People Are Using AI Chatbots to Learn, and That Behavior Is Growing

**Purpose:** Supporting data for Udacity business plan FAQ document on AI-driven learning product
**Audience:** Internal Udacity leadership
**Compiled:** July 2026
**Scope:** Public, verifiable data only. All sources include URLs. Data points are dated.

---

## Summary of Key Findings

The evidence across major industry surveys and credible research is consistent and strong:

- AI chatbot usage for learning is mainstream and accelerating across the general population and especially among tech professionals
- The shift is behavioral: developers are replacing traditional resources (Stack Overflow, documentation, tutorials) with AI tools
- Growth curves are steep: AI tool adoption among developers went from 44% (2023) to 62% (2024) to 84% (2025)
- The preference shift is real but incomplete: AI tools are used opportunistically, not as structured learning programs, which is precisely where product opportunity exists
- Key gaps in AI-only learning (hallucinations, lack of structure, no curriculum, no progression) are well-documented and give Udacity a clear differentiation angle

## Question 1: What Share of People Are Using AI Tools for Learning Purposes?

### General Population

**Finding:** As of June 2026, 49% of U.S. adults use AI chatbots (up from 33% in 2024). The top self-reported use case is information searching (42%), which overlaps significantly with learning.
- **Source:** Pew Research Center, "Americans' Views on AI Chatbots, Smart Devices and AI's Impact"
- **URL:** https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/06/17/americans-and-ai-2026-chatbots-smart-devices-and-views-on-impact/
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Relevance:** Establishes large and growing baseline of U.S. adults using AI chatbots, with learning-adjacent use dominant

**Finding:** 74% of AI users globally report using AI to "learn something new" or "understand a complex topic." For the first time in this survey, learning -- not entertainment -- is the top motivation for using AI.
- **Source:** Google, "Our Life with AI" survey (21,000 participants across 21 countries)
- **URL:** https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/education/our-life-with-ai-2025/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Landmark finding: the primary declared use of AI has shifted to learning globally

**Finding:** 17% of U.S. adults say they used ChatGPT specifically to "learn something new," compared to 20% who used it for work tasks -- placing learning nearly on par with work as a top use case.
- **Source:** OpenAI/economic research on ChatGPT usage (referenced in multiple secondary sources)
- **URL:** https://cdn.openai.com/pdf/a253471f-8260-40c6-a2cc-aa93fe9f142e/economic-research-chatgpt-usage-paper.pdf
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Direct measurement of learning as a primary use case for the most widely used AI tool

**Finding:** 57% of Americans have used generative AI for personal purposes. Among those with a bachelor's degree or higher, the rate is 67%.
- **Source:** Brookings Institution, "How Are Americans Using AI? Evidence from a Nationwide Survey"
- **URL:** https://www.brookings.edu/articles/how-are-americans-using-ai-evidence-from-a-nationwide-survey/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Establishes education-correlated adoption -- highly relevant for Udacity's audience of working professionals seeking advancement

### Tech Professionals Specifically

**Finding:** 37% of developers surveyed said they use AI tools to learn to code (2024). By 2025, this rose to 44% -- a 19% year-over-year increase.
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Annual Developer Survey
- **URL (2024):** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2024/ai
- **URL (2025):** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2025/ai/
- **Date:** 2024 and 2025
- **Relevance:** The most widely cited developer survey, showing direct measurement of AI as a learning tool among 65,000+ developers

**Finding:** 33.1% of developers report "learning new concepts or technologies" as a primary AI use case. An additional 20.8% use AI primarily for "learning about a codebase." Combined, these two learning-oriented tasks represent over half of developers' declared AI use cases.
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2025
- **URL:** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2025/ai/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Demonstrates that learning -- not just code generation -- is already a core use case of AI tools for developers

**Finding:** 65% of developers report "faster learning of new tools and technologies" as one of the top five benefits of using AI in their development workflow.
- **Source:** JetBrains State of Developer Ecosystem 2025 (24,534 developers, 194 countries)
- **URL:** https://blog.jetbrains.com/research/2025/10/state-of-developer-ecosystem-2025/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Learning acceleration is a top-cited benefit, not a side effect, of AI tool adoption

**Finding:** AI tools as a learning source among developers increased from 19% to 27% year-over-year (a 42% jump).
- **Source:** JetBrains State of Developer Ecosystem 2025
- **URL:** https://devecosystem-2025.jetbrains.com/artificial-intelligence
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Specific measurement of developers' shift toward AI as a learning resource

**Finding:** Computer Science students are dramatically overrepresented in Claude.ai usage: they account for 36.8% of student conversations but represent only 5.4% of U.S. degrees. Technical/CS learners are using AI at roughly 7x the rate of their share of the degree population.
- **Source:** Anthropic Education Report: How University Students Use Claude (574,740 anonymized student interactions)
- **URL:** https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-education-report-how-university-students-use-claude
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Confirms that tech learners are the heaviest users of AI for learning -- directly relevant to Udacity's target audience

**Finding:** Among people involved in tech projects, AI chatbot adoption for troubleshooting and technical assistance grew from 40% in Q1 2024 to 45% in Q3 2024. Among beginners (under 3 years of experience), growth was even steeper: from 35% to 42%.
- **Source:** Developer Nation, "The Rise of AI Chatbots: Usage and Problem-Solving Insights"
- **URL:** https://www.developernation.net/blog/the-rise-of-ai-chatbots-usage-and-problem-solving-insights/
- **Date:** Q3 2024
- **Relevance:** Shows fast growth in AI chatbot use for technical learning, and that beginners (Udacity's core audience) are adopting most aggressively

## Question 2: Is That Usage Growing, and How Fast?

### Growth in AI Tool Adoption Among Developers

**Finding:** Developers using AI tools went from 44% (2023) to 62% (2024) to 84% (2025) in the Stack Overflow survey -- a near-doubling in two years.
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Annual Developer Survey
- **URL (2024):** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2024/ai
- **URL (2025):** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2025/ai/
- **Date:** 2023-2025 trend
- **Relevance:** The steepest and most consistent growth curve in any major technology adoption survey

**Finding:** Among professional developers, daily AI usage reached 50.6% in 2025. Early-career developers show even higher daily usage at 55.5%.
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2025
- **URL:** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2025/ai/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Daily usage means AI has become habitual -- a strong signal of behavior change, not experimentation

**Finding:** LinkedIn members showed a 142x increase in adding AI skills (like Copilot and ChatGPT) to their profiles. Use of generative AI at work nearly doubled in just six months in 2024, reaching 75% of global knowledge workers.
- **Source:** Microsoft and LinkedIn 2024 Work Trend Index (31,000 people, 31 countries)
- **URL:** https://news.microsoft.com/source/2024/05/08/microsoft-and-linkedin-release-the-2024-work-trend-index-on-the-state-of-ai-at-work/
- **Date:** May 2024
- **Relevance:** Rate of growth (not just current level) is extraordinary across professional populations

**Finding:** Global GenAI course enrollments on Coursera grew 1,060% year-over-year in 2024. In 2023, one learner enrolled in a GenAI course every minute; by 2024 that rate had quadrupled.
- **Source:** Coursera 2024 Global Skills Report
- **URL:** https://blog.coursera.org/presenting-the-2024-coursera-global-skills-report/
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Explosive demand signal -- people are not just using AI casually, they are actively seeking structured ways to learn about and with AI

**Finding:** WEF Future of Jobs Report 2025 found that of nearly 7.4 million AI enrollments on Coursera in 2024, over 3.2 million were in GenAI training -- an average of six enrollments per minute.
- **Source:** World Economic Forum, Future of Jobs Report 2025
- **URL:** https://www.weforum.org/publications/the-future-of-jobs-report-2025/
- **Date:** January 2025
- **Relevance:** Third-party corroboration of demand surge from the most credible global labor market source

**Finding:** Pew Research found that AI chatbot usage among U.S. adults grew from 33% to 49% between summer 2024 and June 2026 -- a 48% increase in roughly 18 months.
- **Source:** Pew Research Center
- **URL:** https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/06/17/americans-and-ai-2026-chatbots-smart-devices-and-views-on-impact/
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Relevance:** Fastest documented growth in a major technology behavior among U.S. adults in recent memory

### GitHub and Developer Platform Evidence

**Finding:** GitHub saw a 100% increase in teachers, students, and open source maintainers using GitHub Copilot in the complimentary access program (2024). Over 450,000 GitHub Education users made first-time contributions to open source projects in one year.
- **Source:** GitHub Octoverse 2024
- **URL:** https://github.blog/news-insights/octoverse/octoverse-2024/
- **Date:** November 2024
- **Relevance:** Shows AI coding tool adoption accelerating specifically in the learning/education segment

**Finding:** 73% of open source respondents report using AI tools like GitHub Copilot for coding or documentation.
- **Source:** GitHub Octoverse 2024
- **URL:** https://github.blog/news-insights/octoverse/octoverse-2024/
- **Date:** November 2024
- **Relevance:** Near-majority adoption among the developer community most associated with skill sharing and learning

## Question 3: What Specifically Are Tech Professionals Using AI For in Their Learning, and How Often?

### Specific AI Use Cases (Developer Surveys)

**Finding:** Among developers using AI tools (2024 Stack Overflow):
- Writing code: 82%
- Asking questions / getting answers: 68%
- Learning to code: 37% (rising to 44% in 2025)
- Debugging and error resolution: cited as top use for junior developers especially
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2024
- **URL:** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2024/ai
- **Date:** 2024

**Finding:** 49% of developers regularly use ChatGPT for coding. 26% regularly use GitHub Copilot. Both are primarily used for immediate task assistance but also for explaining concepts and learning patterns.
- **Source:** JetBrains State of Developer Ecosystem 2024 (23,262 developers)
- **URL:** https://www.jetbrains.com/lp/devecosystem-2024/
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Establishes ChatGPT as the dominant tool developers reach for when learning, ahead of purpose-built coding assistants

**Finding:** For junior developers specifically, 57% use AI chatbots to debug code and error messages. Other top uses include learning new languages and frameworks, code review and optimization, and understanding documentation.
- **Source:** Multiple sources including index.dev, Stack Overflow, JetBrains (aggregated from public survey data)
- **URL:** https://www.index.dev/blog/chatgpt-statistics
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Confirms that beginners -- Udacity's target learner -- are the heaviest users of AI for learning tasks

**Finding:** Among students using Claude.ai (1 million anonymized conversations analyzed), the top use cases were:
- Content creation and improvement: 39.3%
- Technical explanations and problem-solving: 33.5%
- Data analysis and visualization: 11%
- Research support: 6.5%
- **Source:** Anthropic Education Report (university students)
- **URL:** https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-education-report-how-university-students-use-claude
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Detailed behavioral data on how learners actually use AI -- dominated by technical explanation and problem-solving, not just answer retrieval

**Finding:** 85% of students in AI-using university populations use AI for schoolwork. 78% use it to understand complex topics. MSc students use AI 2.3x more frequently than undergraduates, suggesting usage scales with task complexity and seniority.
- **Source:** Google "Our Life with AI" survey (21,000 participants) + academic research on programming education
- **URL:** https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/education/our-life-with-ai-2025/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Shows that experienced learners seeking advanced knowledge use AI most intensively -- directly relevant to Udacity's professional upskilling audience

**Finding:** Among developers using AI for learning (Stack Overflow 2025), "speed up my learning" was cited as the top benefit by 70.8% of those learning to code -- higher than even productivity gains. This is the single most valued benefit of AI for learners, not professionals.
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2024/2025
- **URL:** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2024/ai
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Speed of learning is the primary value proposition developers already associate with AI -- Udacity should position against this

## Question 4: Are There Signals That People Prefer AI-Assisted Learning Over Traditional Formats?

### Behavioral Evidence: Abandonment of Traditional Developer Learning Resources

**Finding:** Stack Overflow questions dropped 34.8% year-over-year by June 2024, and 40.2% by December 2024 vs. December 2023. Monthly new question volume has collapsed from peaks over 200,000 in 2014 to under 50,000 by late 2025.
- **Source:** Multiple sources; Similarweb traffic data analysis
- **URL:** https://www.similarweb.com/blog/insights/ai-news/stack-overflow-chatgpt/
- **Date:** 2024-2025
- **Relevance:** Most dramatic behavioral signal -- developers have stopped using the world's largest coding Q&A platform at the same time AI tool adoption has surged

**Finding:** Stack Overflow saw a 14% traffic drop in March 2023, just four months after ChatGPT launched. The correlation with ChatGPT's launch was direct: "Developers increasingly get advice from AI chatbots and GitHub CoPilot rather than Stack Overflow."
- **Source:** Similarweb analysis, March 2023
- **URL:** https://www.similarweb.com/blog/insights/ai-news/stack-overflow-chatgpt/
- **Date:** April 2023
- **Relevance:** Shows the substitution effect was immediate and measurable from ChatGPT's first months

**Finding:** A peer-reviewed 2024 study (CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems) estimates a 25% substitution effect -- when ChatGPT is available, Stack Overflow loses a quantifiable share of user participation.
- **Source:** "Is Stack Overflow Obsolete?" (ACM CHI 2024 Conference paper)
- **URL:** https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3613904.3642596
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Academic peer-reviewed confirmation that developers are substituting AI for traditional Q&A forums

### Preference Signals from Survey Data

**Finding:** 72% of all developer respondents are favorable or very favorable about AI tools for development. Only 24.4% don't plan to use AI tools.
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2024
- **URL:** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2024/ai
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Sentiment is positive majority, not a marginal trend

**Finding:** Developers cite these reasons for preferring AI over traditional forums: instant answers with no waiting, conversational and step-by-step guidance, personalization to the user's context and skill level, no judgment (unlike Stack Overflow's downvoting system).
- **Source:** "Why Developers Are Leaving Stack Overflow for AI Tools" (UV Netware, referencing multiple surveys)
- **URL:** https://articles.uvnetware.com/coding/developers-leaving-stack-overflow-ai-tools/
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Qualitative motivations align directly with what learners want: personalization, pace, no embarrassment -- all gaps in traditional online courses

**Finding:** 77% of students use LLMs for programming tasks. 75% use AI tools specifically for debugging. 82% report time savings. 56% report increased motivation.
- **Source:** Systematic review of AI in programming education (29 studies, published in International Journal of Learning, Teaching and Educational Research)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2510.03884v1
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Across multiple academic studies, majority students are using AI, not as a supplement they reluctantly adopted, but as a primary resource

**Finding:** Among K-12 students, 48% use ChatGPT at least weekly (up 27 percentage points in one year). For teachers, 46% now use ChatGPT at least weekly.
- **Source:** CNBC citing survey data on K-12 adoption
- **URL:** https://www.cnbc.com/2024/06/11/ai-is-getting-very-popular-among-students-and-teachers-very-quickly.html
- **Date:** June 2024
- **Relevance:** Establishes early behavioral norms -- younger learners entering Udacity's funnel already expect AI-assisted learning as default

**Finding:** McKinsey found that 7 in 10 employees ignored formal AI onboarding videos, instead relying on experiential learning (trial and error) and peer discussion. This suggests self-directed, conversational learning (exactly what AI tools offer) is more natural to workers than formal structured training.
- **Source:** McKinsey, "AI in the Workplace" report
- **URL:** https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/our-insights/superagency-in-the-workplace-empowering-people-to-unlock-ais-full-potential-at-work
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Workers are already self-directing their AI learning. Udacity's challenge is to make that self-direction more structured and effective.

**Finding:** LinkedIn Learning data shows a 160% increase in non-technical professionals using LinkedIn Learning courses to build AI aptitude. Professionals are not waiting for employer-provided training -- they are upskilling themselves.
- **Source:** Microsoft and LinkedIn 2024 Work Trend Index
- **URL:** https://news.microsoft.com/source/2024/05/08/microsoft-and-linkedin-release-the-2024-work-trend-index-on-the-state-of-ai-at-work/
- **Date:** May 2024
- **Relevance:** Learning about AI with AI tools is a self-motivated behavior driven by career concern, not employer mandate

## Question 5: Where Does AI Learning Fall Short?

This is the most important section for Udacity's business case: the gaps are real, well-documented, and represent a genuine product opportunity.

### Trust and Accuracy Issues

**Finding:** Despite 84% of developers using AI tools, only 29% trust AI output to be accurate (down from 43% in 2024 and 40% in 2023). Trust in AI accuracy is declining even as adoption grows.
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2025
- **URL:** https://stackoverflow.co/company/press/archive/stack-overflow-2025-developer-survey/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** The growing adoption-trust gap is the defining paradox: people use AI but don't fully trust it, which creates demand for human-validated structured learning

**Finding:** 66% of developers say their biggest frustration with AI is "AI solutions that are almost right, but not quite." 45% say debugging AI-generated code is more time-consuming than other code.
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2024 and 2025
- **URL:** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2025/ai/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** "Almost right but not quite" is a fundamental problem in unstructured AI learning: without a structured curriculum, learners cannot tell what they don't know

**Finding:** 79% of developers worry about AI circulating misinformation. 65% prioritize source attribution in AI responses (meaning they don't trust answers without sources).
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2024
- **URL:** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2024/ai
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Learners using AI tools face hallucination risk with no mechanism to know when they've been misled -- a gap structured learning solves

### Over-reliance and Shallow Learning

**Finding:** Academic research found the most widespread concern across 29 studies (present in 65.52% of them) was students "skip[ping] deep learning by copying AI-generated code," resulting in weaker critical thinking and superficial understanding.
- **Source:** Systematic review, "Teaching with AI: A Systematic Review of Chatbots, Generative Tools, and Tutoring Systems in Programming Education" (International Journal of Learning, Teaching and Educational Research)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2510.03884v1
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Structured learning with outcomes and assessment can catch and correct shallow learning; ad-hoc AI use cannot

**Finding:** 24% of students engaged in direct copying of AI-generated solutions rather than learning from them (24% plagiarism rate documented in studies).
- **Source:** Same systematic review (29 studies)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2510.03884v1
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Without structure or accountability, AI tools enable copying rather than learning -- a product opportunity for platforms that incorporate outcome verification

**Finding:** Nearly half (47%) of student-AI interactions on Claude.ai were direct exchanges with minimal engagement -- students asking for answers rather than working through problems collaboratively. Students tend to delegate tasks (42% delegate "creating" to Claude) rather than use AI as a thought partner.
- **Source:** Anthropic Education Report (574,740 student interactions)
- **URL:** https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-education-report-how-university-students-use-claude
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Unstructured AI learning defaults to answer retrieval, not skill building -- exactly what a Udacity-style platform would redirect

### Lack of Structure and Curriculum

**Finding:** Research identifies that AI chatbots provide "unstructured support, contrasting with traditional chatbots' pedagogical design advantages." Custom AI agents "work better than general chatbots like ChatGPT because they can be built with prompts to focus only on relevant course content."
- **Source:** Multiple academic studies; summarized in systematic review of AI in programming education
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2510.03884v1
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** General-purpose AI tools are not pedagogically designed -- they have no curriculum, no progression, no learning objectives, no assessment

**Finding:** Only 3.1% of developers "highly trust" AI output. 45.7% actively distrust it. Even among adopters, 75% would still consult people when they distrust AI answers.
- **Source:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2025
- **URL:** https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2025/ai/
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** The human validation gap -- people know AI can be wrong but have no systematic way to verify learning -- is precisely what structured courses with expert-designed content and assessments address

**Finding:** JetBrains 2025 data shows AI use "isn't systematized. Developers are just using it ad hoc." AI is in pilots and partial rollouts for most organizations rather than embedded in core workflows.
- **Source:** JetBrains State of Developer Ecosystem 2025
- **URL:** https://devecosystem-2025.jetbrains.com/artificial-intelligence
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Ad-hoc learning with AI has real limits for deliberate skill acquisition -- the market is ready for structured AI-assisted learning programs

### The Employer Support Gap

**Finding:** 67% of workers expect employers to help them learn to use AI, but two-thirds say that is not happening. Only 29% of workers globally say their workplace invests enough in AI training. Meanwhile, 54% of workers believe AI will advance their career but 62% say they lack the skills to use it effectively.
- **Source:** Salesforce Research, "New Research: Over Half of Workers Say Generative AI Will Help Advance Their Career"
- **URL:** https://www.salesforce.com/news/stories/generative-ai-skills-research/
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** There is a large, unmet demand for credible AI skill-building that workers cannot get from their employers -- and they are willing to seek it elsewhere

**Finding:** IBM estimates 40% of the workforce will need reskilling due to AI over the next three years. Executives expect 53% of workforces will require upskilling just to perform their current roles effectively.
- **Source:** IBM Institute for Business Value (cited in multiple IBM publications)
- **URL:** https://www.ibm.com/think/insights/ai-upskilling
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Scale of upskilling need dwarfs what any employer alone can provide -- creates systemic demand for external learning platforms

**Finding:** WEF 2025 projects that employers expect 39% of key skills required by the job market will change by 2030. AI and big data top the list of growing skills. 85% of employers are currently offering upskilling, but 77% are providing AI training -- meaning 23% of employers are not.
- **Source:** World Economic Forum, Future of Jobs Report 2025
- **URL:** https://www.weforum.org/publications/the-future-of-jobs-report-2025/
- **Date:** January 2025
- **Relevance:** Confirmed systemic skills gap with employer solutions already proving insufficient to meet demand

## Data Quality Notes and Gaps

### Strong data (multiple independent sources, large sample sizes):
- Developer AI tool adoption rates (Stack Overflow, JetBrains, GitHub Octoverse)
- Learning as a top use case for AI (Google, Stack Overflow, Anthropic)
- Growth rates in adoption (Pew, LinkedIn, Coursera)
- Trust deficits and frustrations (Stack Overflow 2024 and 2025)

### Moderate data (credible but fewer corroborating sources):
- Specific percentage of developers using AI for structured learning vs. ad-hoc queries
- Preference for AI over traditional courses (behavioral signal is clear from Stack Overflow traffic; explicit preference data is thinner)
- Learning outcomes comparing AI-only vs. structured programs (mostly from academic settings, not professional upskilling)

### Weak or missing data:
- Longitudinal data on whether AI-only learners achieve comparable job outcomes to credentialed learners (this would be the strongest data point for Udacity's value proposition, but it does not yet exist at scale)
- Specific data on tech professionals comparing AI chatbots to online courses (most survey data conflates these)
- HolonIQ report primary data (paywalled; only summaries publicly available)

### Note on date range:
The bulk of strong data is from 2024-2025, which is appropriate given that the AI learning behavior shift is recent. Data from 2023 is included where it establishes the baseline or shows growth trajectory.
