# LLM for Learning: Research Review
### Data supporting: "People are using AI chatbots to learn, and that behavior is growing"

Prepared for: Anastasia | Date: July 2026 | Sources: 25+ verified public sources

---

## The Short Version

The data is strong. People -- especially tech professionals -- are already using AI chatbots as their primary learning tool. Adoption is accelerating fast. And the research also clearly documents *why this creates a product opportunity*: the very same people who use AI to learn don't trust it, find it shallow, and have no structured path forward. That gap is exactly what LLM for Learning fills.

---

## 1. People Are Using AI to Learn

**74%** of AI users globally now cite learning as their top reason for using AI -- for the first time, learning beat out entertainment and utility tasks as the primary motivation.
*Source: Google "Our Life with AI" survey, 21,000 people across 21 countries, 2025*

**44%** of developers used AI specifically to learn to code in 2025, up from 37% the year before.
*Source: Stack Overflow Developer Survey, 65,000+ developers, 2025*

**33%** of developers say "learning new concepts or technologies" is their primary AI use case. Another 21% say "learning a new codebase." Together, learning-oriented tasks account for more than half of all declared AI use among developers.
*Source: Stack Overflow Developer Survey, 2025*

**85%** of students in AI-using university programs use AI to understand complex topics. MSc-level students (more advanced, closer to Udacity's audience) use AI 2.3x more than undergraduates.
*Source: Google, 2025*

**CS learners specifically** are dramatically overrepresented in AI usage: they account for 36.8% of student conversations on Claude.ai but only 5.4% of U.S. degrees. Tech learners use AI at roughly 7x the rate of their share of the student population.
*Source: Anthropic Education Report, 574,740 anonymized student interactions, 2025*

---

## 2. The Growth Rate Is Exceptional

Developer AI tool adoption went from **44% (2023) → 62% (2024) → 84% (2025)**. Near-doubling in two years. Daily AI usage among professional developers is now 50.6%. Among early-career developers it is 55.5%.
*Source: Stack Overflow, 2023-2025*

AI tools as a learning source among developers increased **42% in one year** (from 19% to 27%).
*Source: JetBrains State of Developer Ecosystem, 24,534 developers, 2025*

GenAI course enrollments on Coursera grew **1,060% year-over-year** in 2024. One learner enrolled in a GenAI course every 15 seconds by the end of 2024.
*Source: Coursera Global Skills Report, 2024; World Economic Forum, 2025*

U.S. adult AI chatbot usage grew from **33% to 49%** in roughly 18 months.
*Source: Pew Research Center, 2024-2026*

LinkedIn members showed a **142x increase** in adding AI skills to their profiles. Generative AI use at work nearly doubled in six months, reaching 75% of global knowledge workers.
*Source: Microsoft and LinkedIn Work Trend Index, 31,000 people across 31 countries, 2024*

---

## 3. Tech Professionals Are the Leading Edge

Developers are not just using AI -- they are replacing traditional learning resources with it entirely:

Stack Overflow questions dropped **40% year-over-year** by the end of 2024. Monthly question volume has collapsed from 200,000 in 2014 to under 50,000 in 2025. The timing correlates directly with ChatGPT's launch.
*Source: Similarweb analysis; peer-reviewed ACM CHI 2024 paper (25% substitution effect confirmed)*

**73%** of open source developers now use AI tools for coding and documentation.
*Source: GitHub Octoverse, 2024*

**49%** of developers regularly use ChatGPT for coding. Among junior developers, **57%** use AI chatbots specifically to debug and learn from error messages.
*Source: JetBrains Developer Ecosystem, 2024*

**70.8%** of developers learning to code with AI say the single biggest benefit is speed of learning -- higher than productivity gains, higher than time savings.
*Source: Stack Overflow, 2024*

---

## 4. But There Is a Clear Gap -- and That Is the Opportunity

This is the most important section for the business case. The same people who use AI to learn do not fully trust it, find it incomplete, and have no structured path.

**Only 29% of developers trust AI output to be accurate** -- down from 43% in 2024, even as adoption grows to 84%. Trust is falling as adoption rises.
*Source: Stack Overflow Developer Survey, 2025*

**65% of developers** say their biggest frustration is "AI solutions that are almost right, but not quite." 79% worry about AI circulating misinformation.
*Source: Stack Overflow, 2024-2025*

**65.5% of academic studies** on AI in programming education cite "shallow learning" as the primary concern -- students skip deep understanding by copying AI-generated answers.
*Source: Systematic review of 29 studies, 2025*

**47% of student-AI interactions** are direct answer exchanges with minimal learning engagement. Students delegate 42% of their "creating" tasks to AI rather than using it as a thinking partner.
*Source: Anthropic Education Report, 574,740 interactions, 2025*

Academic research is unambiguous: **"Custom AI agents work better than general chatbots like ChatGPT because they can be built with prompts to focus only on relevant course content."** General-purpose AI has no curriculum, no progression, no learning objectives, no assessment.
*Source: Systematic review of AI in programming education, 2025*

---

## 5. The Market Need Is Confirmed by Employer Data

**67% of workers** expect employers to help them learn AI skills. Two-thirds say that is not happening.
*Source: Salesforce Research, 2024*

**54%** of workers believe AI will advance their career. **62%** say they lack the skills to use it effectively.
*Source: Salesforce Research, 2024*

IBM estimates **40% of the workforce** will need reskilling due to AI in the next three years. Yet only 29% of workers globally say their workplace invests enough in AI training.

WEF 2025 projects that **39% of key skills** required by the job market will change by 2030, with AI and big data at the top of the list.
*Source: World Economic Forum Future of Jobs Report, 2025*

---

## What the Data Supports (and What It Doesn't)

**Strong data (multiple independent large-scale sources):**
- AI tool adoption rates among developers
- Learning as a top declared use case
- Explosive growth curves
- Trust deficits and frustrations
- Employer skills gap and unmet demand

**Moderate data (credible but fewer sources):**
- Explicit preference for AI over traditional courses (behavioral signal from Stack Overflow traffic is clear; explicit preference surveys are thinner)
- Learning outcome comparisons between AI-only and structured programs

**Missing data (honest gaps):**
- Longitudinal data showing whether AI-only learners achieve comparable job outcomes to credentialed learners -- this does not yet exist at scale and would be the most powerful argument for Udacity's value proposition
